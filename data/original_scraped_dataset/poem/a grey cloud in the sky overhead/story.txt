A grey cloud, in the sky overhead, Like a squirrel skin uncurled. 'I'm not sorry your body,' he said, 'Will melt in March, frail snow-girl!'  In the soft muff my hands grew cold. Ifelt afraid, somehow confused. How to recall the swift weeks' flow, His short-lived insubstantial love!  I don't want bitterness or revenge, Let me die with the last snow-storm.
