For Vasily Komarovsky  Such strange words That quiet April day brought me. You knew it was still alive in me, That dreadful week of passion.  I heard no pealing of bells, Floating in clear azure, For seven days copper laughter chimed, Silvery sorrow streamed.  And I, veiling my face, As if for eternal parting, Lay, awaiting there The still-nameless torment.\n\n\t\n\n\t\n\n\t\n\n\tReturn to the Anna Akhmatova library , or . . . Read the next poem; Solitude
